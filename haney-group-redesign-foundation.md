# Haney Group Website Redesign Foundation

> A planning document for the redesign of **https://www.haney-group.com**. Extracts the current design and content system from the live site, analyzes positioning gaps, and recommends a new architecture, messaging framework, and design direction aimed at converting more qualified Texas government-affairs prospects.
>
> Sections labeled **"Recommendation"** reflect proposed direction rather than current site content.

---

## 1. Current Site Overview

The current Haney Group website is a four-page brochure site for a small, principal-led Texas lobbying and government affairs firm based in Austin. Its purpose is to introduce the firm, communicate that the principals have deep Texas Capitol experience, and invite a prospective client to "Start Making an Impact" by filling out a contact form.

- **Purpose.** Establish credibility for a boutique, relationship-driven lobby firm; differentiate on parliamentary expertise and legislative messaging.
- **Primary audience.** Government affairs decision-makers at trade associations, regulated companies, and large institutions that need representation at the Texas Legislature.
- **Main services presented.** Lobbying / legislative advocacy, policy strategy, coalition building, communications and messaging, stakeholder education ("Legislature 101"), and consulting to legislative bodies on modernizing parliamentary procedure.
- **Business development goal.** Generate inbound contact-form inquiries from prospective Texas clients and reassure referred prospects that the firm is established, credible, and worth a meeting.

The site achieves brand presence but does not yet operate as a true business-development asset: it is light on proof, light on issue-area depth, and built around generic "voice at the Capitol" language rather than the specific outcomes a buyer is trying to procure.

### 2.4 Contact Us

- **Title / hero.** "Contact us"
- **Content summary.** A four-field form (Name*, Message, E-mail, GDPR-style consent checkbox) above a "Get in touch" block listing Telephone **512-925-5000**, e-mail **info@haney-group.com**, and address **P.O. Box 521, Austin, TX 78767**. A photograph of a Capitol-era room appears to the right.

- **Calls to action.** **"Send"** submit button.

- **Weaknesses.** No subject-line dropdown to route inquiries; no qualification fields (organization, role, issue area); no expected response time; no calendar / scheduling option; no map; the GDPR consent line is legalistic and Eurocentric for a Texas firm; Robert's empty headshot slot in About propagates a sense of "site not finished."
- **Missing opportunities.** Could add "Schedule a confidential consultation," propose a 20-minute intro call, segment by client type ("I represent a city / association / company / nonprofit"), or offer a downloadable "What to expect when you hire a Texas lobbying firm" PDF as a lead magnet.

### 2.5 Site-wide / repeated patterns

- The same CTA ("Start Making an Impact") repeats on every page.
- The tagline **"Proven Expertise. Focused Results. Unmatched Influence."** appears in the footer of every page.
- Every page uses the navy / black palette and the same serif headline + sans body pairing.
- The overflow "…" in the nav suggests a hidden / unbuilt page slot (likely an Insights or Blog placeholder).

## 3. Current Brand and Design System

### 3.1 Logo

- A monoline **Texas Capitol dome icon** above the wordmark **"THE HANEY GROUP"** in a serif-display weight.
- Used in white-on-navy in the header and in white at large scale at the bottom of every page as a signature.
- Variation: in the page footer the logo is reproduced very large, almost as a watermark / sign-off device.

### 3.2 Color palette (approximate)

| Role | Hex (approx.) | Usage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Primary navy | `#101A38` – `#15224A` | Header, dominant page background, footer |
| Deep slate panel | `#1E2A52` | Secondary content bands |
| Near-black panel | `#0A0E1B` | High-contrast bands (stats, mid-page focal blocks) |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | All body and headline text on dark; full-bleed client-logo band |
| Light periwinkle / lavender | `#B9C4F2` – `#A8B5F0` | Button fills and active nav pill |
| Muted body gray-blue | `#C8D0E3` | Secondary body copy on navy |

The overall palette reads serious, institutional, "Capitol after-dark." Recommendation: keep navy as the brand anchor but introduce a warmer accent (Texas-bluebonnet purple, burnt-orange, or a deep gold) for CTAs and section accents so buttons read as decisively "click here" rather than as a softer secondary element.

### 3.3 Typography

- **Display / headlines.** A high-contrast transitional serif (visually consistent with **Playfair Display**, **Cormorant**, or a Didone-adjacent face), set bold, with tight line-height. Used for page titles, section headings, and the wordmark.
- **Body.** A clean humanist sans-serif (reads like **Lato**, **Source Sans 3**, or **Inter**), regular weight, with generous line-height. Used for paragraph copy and nav.
- **Small caps / nav.** Sans regular, slightly tracked.

There is one hierarchy level (giant serif → medium serif subhead → sans body). Recommendation: add an explicit eyebrow / kicker style and a defined data style for stats so trust signals can stand out without inflating headline size.

### 3.4 Buttons

- Pill-shaped, light periwinkle fill, dark navy text, no border, no shadow.
- Single button style across the site (primary only).
- Nav: active page indicated by the same pill in a slightly different shade.

Recommendation: define **primary** (high-contrast accent), **secondary** (outlined on navy), and **tertiary text-link** styles so the page can present multiple CTAs without visual conflict (e.g., "Schedule a Consultation" vs. "See Our Work").

### 3.5 Cards

- Services are presented as **circular framed images** above a heading and short paragraph — closer to a magazine vignette than a clickable card.
- No drop shadow, no border, no hover state evident.
- Stat cards are simply large numerals + caption.

Recommendation: introduce true cards (rectangle, soft border, subtle elevation, hover lift) for service summaries that link to deeper pages.

### 3.6 Section layouts

- Full-bleed navy or black bands stacked vertically.
- Two-column layouts where one column is text and the other is intentionally empty (negative space), giving an editorial / wide feel but also leaving large dead zones.
- Content is left-anchored on most bands.

Recommendation: reserve the half-empty layout for hero and quote bands; fill secondary bands with proof imagery (Capitol photography, team photos, charts of legislation handled).

### 3.7 Header and footer

- **Header.** Logo left, nav right, navy background, sticky on scroll. Active-page pill.
- **Footer.** Repeat of nav, copyright line, tagline, three social icons, and a very large centered logo treatment.

Recommendation: footer should also surface the phone number, Austin address, "Confidential inquiry" CTA, and a small site map (Services / Industries / Insights / About / Contact).

### 3.8 Imagery and icons

- Real photography: Capitol architecture (House chamber, rotunda, chandeliers, columns), a podium shot of Robert, and a portrait of Julie.
- Photographs are masked into circles in services and team blocks.
- No iconography beyond social icons and the logo's dome mark.

Recommendation: develop a small set of custom line icons keyed to issue areas (capitol-dome for state policy, gavel for parliamentary, water drop for infrastructure, caduceus for healthcare, etc.) to give the site a system rather than an album.

### 3.9 Spacing, borders, shadows, visual rhythm

- Generous vertical padding between bands; minimal use of borders or dividers.
- Thin horizontal rule under each page title (a single white hairline).
- No shadows or depth — entirely flat.
- Rhythm is set by alternating band colors rather than by typographic scale.

### 3.10 Tone and feel

Stately, institutional, Capitol-after-dark, serious. Reads more like a museum or law-firm site than a contemporary advisory firm. The lack of motion, hover states, and modern interaction patterns can make the site feel underbuilt despite a clean palette.

## 4. Current Messaging and Positioning

### 4.1 Primary value proposition

> *"The Haney Group specializes in government relations, legislative consulting, and advocacy at the Texas Capitol. Our professional services are designed to maximize your impact across a variety of policy areas."*

Supported by the hero: *"We'll Make Sure Your Voice Is Heard and Your Interests Are Front and Center."*

This is a category statement ("we are a lobby firm") rather than a positioning statement ("we are the firm you hire when…"). It does not distinguish the firm from any other Austin shop.

### 4.2 Supporting proof points

- **40+ years** combined Texas Capitol experience.
- **15+ years** recognized nationally as a parliamentary expert.
- **20+ years** in legislative communications and strategic messaging.
- Robert Haney's tenure as **Chief Clerk of the Texas House** under five Speakers; "Rising Star" recognition by *Texas Capitol Inside* (2023).
- Julie Haney's experience with the **Chair of House Appropriations** and as **Chief of Staff** to a state legislator.
- Client logos: **Texas REALTORS**, **Blue Cross Blue Shield of Texas**, **Sports Betting Alliance**, **Texas Managed Care Alliance**.
- Consulting work with **state legislatures across the U.S.** to modernize parliamentary procedure.

### 4.3 Tone of voice

Formal, institutional, second-person ("your voice," "your interests," "your agenda"), reassurance-oriented, and somewhat hedged ("we'll make sure," "designed to maximize"). Tends toward Capitol-flavored marketing language rather than client-outcome language.

### 4.4 How credibility is established

- Named principals with specific résumés (Chief Clerk, Appropriations, Chief of Staff).
- Recognizable Texas client logos.
- Quantified experience bands.
- Capitol photography.

### 4.5 How experience is presented

Through years-of-experience numerals, biographical paragraphs, and a single attributed quote. There are **no case studies, no named bills, no committees, no quotable client testimonials, and no outcomes** ("passed," "defeated," "amended," "secured appropriation of…").

### 4.6 Does the site explain why a prospective client should hire The Haney Group?

Partially. It explains *what kind of firm* The Haney Group is (Texas lobbying, broad-based) and *who is behind it* (an unusually procedural-savvy team). It does **not** explain *what problem a client should hire it to solve* — e.g., "passing healthcare bills in the appropriations process," "defending against unfavorable preemption," "representing a city before legislative committees," "navigating an interim study."

### 4.7 Language that feels generic, underdeveloped, or too passive

- "Maximize your impact across a variety of policy areas" — buzzy and uncommitted.
- "Voice is heard" / "interests are front and center" — appears twice on the homepage; both pageviews close with the same idea.
- "Comprehensive representation" — every firm says this.
- "Top-notch legislative consulting services" — promotional, weakens authority.
- "We'll guide you through every step of the process" — descriptive, not differentiating.
- "Modernizing Legislative Processes" service is the firm's most novel offering and is buried in position 6 of 7.

## 5. Target Client Interpretation

The current site already serves four very different buyer types (a trade association, a national health-insurance carrier, a gaming-industry coalition, and a healthcare-coalition trade group). Below are inferred target audiences and what each needs to see to feel confident submitting an inquiry.

### 5.1 Trade and professional associations (e.g., Texas REALTORS, TMCA)

- **Needs to see:** named former-association clients; understanding of board-driven decision-making; "Legislature 101" board-training capability; ability to manage a coalition; track record on member-priority bills.
- **Conversion cue:** "We represent statewide associations whose boards need to see results between sessions and during them."

### 5.2 Health care entities (insurers, hospital systems, managed-care organizations, physicians)

- **Needs to see:** appropriations-process fluency; HHSC and TDI relationships; understanding of Medicaid, scope-of-practice, network-adequacy, and 1115 waiver dynamics; named health bills tracked or moved.
- **Conversion cue:** "Health care policy in Texas runs through Appropriations, HHSC, and a small number of committees. We work all three."

### 5.3 Cities, counties, and special districts (water, transit, MUDs, hospital districts, ESDs)

- **Needs to see:** non-preemption / home-rule advocacy; bill-review and amendment work; ability to testify and prepare officials to testify; experience with House Land & Resource Management, House County Affairs, Senate Local Government, Senate Water.
- **Conversion cue:** "Local governments hire us when state legislation threatens local authority or when a district needs an appropriations or statutory fix."

### 5.4 Infrastructure / transportation / water / energy

- **Needs to see:** rate-case, certificate, permitting, and TCEQ familiarity; appropriations work for SWIFT, TIRZ, or transportation funding; named bills tracked at TXDOT, PUC, and Railroad Commission.
- **Conversion cue:** "Capital-intensive infrastructure depends on both the budget and the statutory framework. We work both."

### 5.5 Private companies with Texas legislative needs (tech, retail, telecom, manufacturing, gaming, financial services)

- **Needs to see:** issue ownership rather than relationship-only positioning; defensive readiness ("if a bad bill drops on day 30, who calls the chair?"); communications support if an issue goes public.
- **Conversion cue:** "When your business model is on the bill calendar, we are the firm you want on the front row."

### 5.6 Nonprofits and foundations

- **Needs to see:** affordability; appropriations-rider work; coalition framing; willingness to do unglamorous interim work.
- **Conversion cue:** "We work with mission-driven organizations whose biggest annual decision is the state budget."

### 5.7 National / out-of-state interests with Texas exposure

- **Needs to see:** orientation to Texas-specific process and personalities; ability to serve as the "boots on the ground" for a D.C. or national government-affairs team.
- **Conversion cue:** "We are the Texas desk for national government-affairs operations."

## 6. Competitive Redesign Opportunities

The most consequential redesign moves, in priority order:

1. **Reposition the homepage.** Move from "Are your interests being overlooked?" (generic empathy) to a positioning statement that names the firm's edge — procedural mastery + appropriations + communications under one roof. Lead with what kind of client the firm wins for.
2. **Group services into three meaningful pillars** rather than seven flat tiles: **Legislative Strategy & Advocacy** · **Appropriations & Budget** · **Public Affairs & Communications** (with a separate institutional-consulting page for the ASLCS / modernization work).
3. **Make Robert's Capitol experience the headline credential**, with a clear "What this means for you" panel: he doesn't just know the process — he ran the process. Add the missing Robert headshot.
4. **Make Julie's appropriations and communications work a co-equal credential** with a dedicated section explaining the practical effect: budget riders, strategic messaging, board-level translation.
5. **Add issue-area / industry pages** (Health Care, Local Government & Special Districts, Infrastructure & Water, Real Estate, Gaming, Associations, Nonprofits). Each is a discoverable SEO landing and a credible match-target for inbound search.
6. **Add a "Selected Engagements" or "Recent Work" section** with anonymized case study cards: situation, role, outcome. Even three is transformative.
7. **Add testimonials and pull quotes from clients**, on-the-record where possible (association executives are usually willing).
8. **Replace the single "Start Making an Impact" CTA** with a two-track CTA system: **"Schedule a confidential consultation"** (qualified prospects) and **"Get our session-in-review briefing"** (top-of-funnel, lead magnet).
9. **Add trust signals** — press mentions, civic / professional roles (ASLCS leadership, recognition from *Capitol Inside*, *Texas Lobby Watch* mentions, association memberships).
10. **Improve mobile presentation** — the current desktop layout's empty half-columns become awkward stacked white space on mobile; rework to a stacked, dense, scannable mobile flow.
11. **Establish SEO structure** — page titles include "Texas lobbying," "Texas government affairs," "Austin lobbying firm," and "Texas legislative consulting"; create issue-area landing pages targeting "Texas healthcare lobbyist," "Texas water lobbyist," "Texas local government lobbyist," etc.
12. **Build an Insights / Session Briefing channel.** Short, high-quality posts during session (weekly) and interim (monthly) earn organic search and give referral sources something to forward.

## 7. Recommended New Site Architecture

Proposed primary nav: **Home · About · Services · Industries · Experience · Insights · Contact**.

### 7.1 Home

- **Purpose.** Convert a referred prospect within 60 seconds and convert an organic visitor within three scrolls.
- **Audience.** All target audience groups (Section 5).
- **Sections.** Hero positioning + lead CTA; credibility band (clients + stats + press); three-pillar services overview; "Why The Haney Group" with named principals; issue-area entry grid; recent engagement highlights; testimonials; close CTA.
- **CTAs.** Primary: "Schedule a Confidential Consultation." Secondary: "See How We Help [Cities · Associations · Health Care · Infrastructure]."
- **SEO targets.** "Texas lobbying firm," "Texas government affairs firm," "Austin lobbying firm."

### 7.2 About

- **Purpose.** Convert credibility into trust by deepening the principal bios and the firm's institutional story.
- **Audience.** Buyers doing diligence after a referral.
- **Sections.** Firm story; principal profiles (Robert and Julie, each with headshot, full bio, Capitol résumé, LinkedIn); approach / values; civic and professional service; press mentions.
- **CTAs.** "Schedule a Consultation," "Read Our Recent Work."
- **SEO targets.** "Robert Haney lobbyist," "Julie Haney lobbyist," "former Chief Clerk Texas House lobbyist."

### 7.3 Services (overview)

- **Purpose.** Anchor the firm's offering in three named pillars.
- **Audience.** Procurement-style buyers comparing firms.
- **Sections.** Pillar 1: Legislative Strategy & Advocacy. Pillar 2: Appropriations & Budget Strategy. Pillar 3: Public Affairs, Communications & Stakeholder Education. Sidebar / fourth: Parliamentary & Procedural Consulting (institutional clients).
- **CTAs.** Deep-link into each service page; "Talk to Us About Your Issue."
- **SEO targets.** "Texas legislative consulting services."

### 7.4 Legislative Strategy & Advocacy

- **Purpose.** Show how the firm runs an offensive *or* defensive bill effort.
- **Audience.** Issue-driven clients (associations, companies, coalitions).
- **Sections.** Bill strategy; committee work; testimony preparation; member relationships; coalition building; interim work.
- **CTAs.** "Discuss Your Legislative Goal."
- **SEO targets.** "Texas legislative strategy," "Texas Capitol lobbying."

### 7.5 Parliamentary & Procedural Consulting

- **Purpose.** Surface Robert's national differentiator — institutional and legislative-body consulting — and signal procedural depth to private clients.
- **Audience.** State legislatures, clerks' offices, parliamentary bodies, and procedurally complex private-client engagements.
- **Sections.** Modernization work; chamber technology and rules; ASLCS leadership; what procedural fluency means inside a private-client engagement.
- **CTAs.** "Consult on a Procedural Project."
- **SEO targets.** "Legislative modernization consulting," "state legislature parliamentary consulting."

### 7.6 Appropriations & Budget Strategy

- **Purpose.** Stake a claim on the most consequential biennial process in Texas.
- **Audience.** Health care, higher education, local governments, nonprofits, infrastructure, agencies seeking riders or line items.
- **Sections.** Budget cycle overview; rider strategy; LBB and agency engagement; interim hearings; appropriations communications.
- **CTAs.** "Talk to Us About a Budget Priority."
- **SEO targets.** "Texas appropriations lobbyist," "Texas budget rider strategy."

### 7.7 Local Government & Special Districts

- **Purpose.** Capture cities, counties, MUDs, ESDs, hospital districts, transit authorities, and water districts.
- **Audience.** City managers, council members, GMs, district counsel.
- **Sections.** Preemption defense; bracket bills; statutory fixes; appropriations support; testimony preparation; interim engagement.
- **CTAs.** "Brief Us on Your Local Issue."
- **SEO targets.** "Texas local government lobbyist," "Texas special district lobbyist," "Texas water district lobbying."

### 7.8 Health Care & Infrastructure

(Recommendation: split into two pages if content allows.)

- **Purpose.** Show issue-area depth in two of the firm's strongest verticals.
- **Audience.** Insurers, hospital systems, managed-care orgs, physician groups; transportation, energy, water, broadband.
- **Sections (Health Care).** Medicaid; managed care; scope-of-practice; network adequacy; HHSC; appropriations work.
- **Sections (Infrastructure).** Transportation; water; broadband; PUC; TCEQ; capital appropriations.
- **CTAs.** "Discuss a Health Care Issue" / "Discuss an Infrastructure Issue."
- **SEO targets.** "Texas healthcare lobbyist," "Texas Medicaid policy lobbyist," "Texas infrastructure lobbyist," "Texas water lobbying firm."

### 7.9 Industries / Clients We Serve

- **Purpose.** Audience-routing landing page.
- **Audience.** All seven target segments (Section 5).
- **Sections.** Card grid by industry, each linking to a deeper page.
- **CTAs.** "Talk to Us About Your Industry."

### 7.10 Experience (Selected Engagements)

- **Purpose.** Replace "trust us" with "look at what we've done."
- **Audience.** Diligence-mode buyers.
- **Sections.** 6–12 anonymized engagement cards: client type, issue, role, outcome; client logo wall; press hits; quotes.
- **CTAs.** "Discuss a Similar Engagement."
- **SEO targets.** "Texas lobbying firm results," "Texas legislative wins."

### 7.11 Insights

- **Purpose.** Earn referral and search traffic between sessions.
- **Audience.** Association executives, GR professionals, journalists, and procurement-mode buyers checking the firm's perspective.
- **Sections.** Session briefings; issue explainers; interim outlook; appropriations roundups; subscribe box.
- **CTAs.** "Subscribe to the Session Briefing."
- **SEO targets.** Every legislative-session topic the firm chooses to write on.

### 7.12 Contact

- **Purpose.** Convert with confidence and segment the inquiry.
- **Audience.** All.
- **Sections.** Confidential-inquiry form with organization type, issue area, urgency; phone; Austin address; principal direct emails; optional consultation scheduler.
- **CTAs.** "Send a Confidential Inquiry" / "Schedule a 20-Minute Consultation."
- **SEO targets.** "Contact Texas lobbying firm," "Haney Group contact."

## 11. Technical and Design Recommendations

### 11.1 Design style

- Evolve the navy/serif palette rather than replace it — it is a real asset for a Capitol firm.
- Introduce a warmer accent (suggest a deep gold or a Texas-burnt-orange, used sparingly on CTAs, eyebrow text, and stat numerals).
- Establish a proper type scale (display, H1–H4, body, small, eyebrow) and stick to it.
- Add real cards with subtle elevation and hover states; add motion on hover and section-enter (subtle fades only).
- Restore the missing Robert Haney headshot; commission new principal photography in consistent lighting and frame.

### 11.2 Mobile-first

- Single-column stack with dense, scannable sections — no half-empty rows.
- Sticky bottom-bar CTA on mobile ("Call · Email · Schedule").
- Tap-friendly hit areas; minimum 44pt buttons.
- Test all hero copy at 360px width to avoid orphaned words.

### 11.3 Accessibility (target WCAG 2.2 AA)

- Maintain ≥ 4.5:1 contrast across all navy backgrounds (current light-periwinkle button may need verification).
- Visible focus states on every interactive element.
- Semantic landmarks, skip-to-content, descriptive `alt` text on Capitol imagery and principal photos.
- Form labels associated with inputs; error states announced to screen readers.
- Captions and transcripts on any video.

### 11.4 Performance

- Lighthouse ≥ 90 on mobile.
- Compress and serve Capitol photography in modern formats (AVIF / WebP) with explicit dimensions.
- Self-host fonts with `font-display: swap`; subset to Latin.
- Lazy-load below-the-fold imagery.
- Avoid third-party chat widgets unless explicitly used by the firm.

### 11.5 CMS or static site

- **Recommended:** a static site framework with a lightweight headless CMS so Insights can be authored without a developer. Suggested stacks: **Next.js + Sanity / Contentful** on Vercel; or **Astro + Decap CMS** on Netlify. Either gives the firm fast performance and easy publishing.
- Avoid a heavy WordPress build unless the firm wants editor-led publishing and is comfortable maintaining plugins.

### 11.6 Imagery / icon direction

- Continue using Capitol-specific photography but commission a unified shoot (rotunda, House chamber, Speaker's rostrum, exterior at golden hour) so the visual library is consistent.
- Add high-quality principal portraits in matching style.
- Build a small custom icon set for the issue-area cards.
- Treat client logos as a first-class trust signal — single row on homepage, grid on Experience page.

### 11.7 Forms and contact strategy

- Replace the single "Send" form with a segmented inquiry form (organization, role, issue area, urgency, brief description).
- Offer a **Calendly / Cal.com** "Schedule a 20-minute confidential consultation" option for qualified prospects.
- Pipe submissions to a CRM (HubSpot, Pipedrive, or Folk) with auto-routing to the relevant principal.
- Add a soft secondary form: "Subscribe to the Session Briefing."

### 11.8 Analytics and conversion tracking

- GA4 with custom events: `cta_schedule`, `cta_call`, `cta_insights_subscribe`, `service_page_view`, `industry_page_view`.
- Conversion-tracking goals on consultation requests, scheduling completions, and newsletter sign-ups.
- Consider Microsoft Clarity (free) for session recordings and heatmaps.
- Quarterly review of which industry pages and Insights posts produce inquiries.
